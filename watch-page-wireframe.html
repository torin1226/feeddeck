Design Decision Notes

Why Labeled Related Sections

YouTube shows related videos with zero context. You never know why a video appears. FeedDeck labels every section: "More from [creator]", "Because you watch [tag]", "Trending in [category]". This builds trust AND helps you skip sections you don't care about.

Up Next Card

Separated from the related list with its own accent-bordered container. Shows countdown timer during end-of-video auto-advance. Queue items override related suggestions here. The accent top-border provides a visual anchor for "this is what plays next."

Player Sizing

Player is NOT full-viewport. Content peeks below the fold. This was the core problem with theatre mode: hiding everything behind the video. The player should be ~60-65vh on a 1080p display, leaving room for info and sidebar context.

Action Bar as Pills

Not icon-only (confusing), not full buttons (heavy). Pill-shaped with icon + short label hits the middle. Consistent with the tag row styling. Liked state uses accent-muted background instead of a fill change — subtler than YouTube's solid blue.

Ambient Player Glow

Option A adds a subtle radial gradient behind the player container. Not prominent — just enough to give the player area depth and separation from the rest of the page. Pulls the accent color at very low opacity. Delete if it feels gimmicky.

Source Badge

Every video shows its source domain as a colored badge (YouTube, PornHub, RedGifs, etc.) in the metadata row. This is unique to FeedDeck as a multi-source aggregator. Users should always know where a video came from. source-badge class uses accent-muted treatment.

Layout Comparison

Dimension A: YouTube Evolved B: Immersive Cinema C: Minimal Focus
Player width ~65% of viewport 100% of viewport ~900px max centered
Related visibility Always visible (sidebar) Below fold only Below fold only
Discovery potential High — sidebar always tempts you Medium — need to scroll Medium — grid below
Viewing focus Good — player dominates left Best — full-width immersion Good — centered, no distractions
Content density High — info + related visible together Medium Low — spacious, editorial
Mobile adaptation Sidebar stacks below Identical to mobile Grid collapses to 2-col naturally
Best for Discovery + binge sessions Lean-back, focused viewing Intentional, curated watching
Closest to YouTube Netflix / Apple TV+ Letterboxd / Vimeo
Recommendation PRIMARY LAYOUT Fullscreen mode variant Not recommended (low discovery)

Recommendation

Go with Option A as default, Option B as "Theatre Mode"

Option A maximizes discovery, which is the whole point of FeedDeck. The sidebar with labeled related sections is the differentiator. Option B is the right layout for when someone hits fullscreen or wants to go lean-back — it can be a toggle state, not a separate page. Option C is too sparse for a content discovery app; it's beautiful but actively works against binge behavior.

Mobile wireframe works for all three options since it's single-column regardless. The sticky player, vertical action bar, and Up Next card pattern are consistent across layouts.
